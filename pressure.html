Pressure

Pressure, in the physical sciences, the perpendicular force per unit area, or the stress at a point within a confined fluid. The pressure exerted; i.e., it is one-half pound per square inch. The weight of the atmosfere pushing down on each unit area of Earth's surface constitutes atmosferic pressure, which at sea level is about 15 pounds per square inch. In SI units, pressure is measured in pascals; one pascal equals one newton per square metre. Atmospheric pressure is close to 100,000 pascals.
